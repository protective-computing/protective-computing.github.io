Protective Computing

Protective Computing is a systems-engineering discipline focused on software designed for conditions of human vulnerability, including crisis, displacement, illness, coercion, and institutional instability.

Foundational Work

  • The Overton Framework: Protective Computing in Conditions of Human Vulnerability (v1.3)
  • Zenodo Community — Protective Computing

Core Principles

  • Reversibility
  • Exposure Minimization
  • Local Authority
  • Coercion Resistance
  • Degraded-Infrastructure Functionality

Scope

Protective Computing intersects with crisis informatics, resilience engineering, safety-critical human–computer interaction, privacy engineering, and digital sovereignty research.

Purpose

Modern software architecture assumes stability. Protective Computing rejects this assumption and advances design principles oriented toward containment, essential utility, and human safety under adverse conditions.
